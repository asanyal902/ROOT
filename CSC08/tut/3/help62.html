2 My First TSelector

One of the experiment's students is a notorious party animal. One day he had to fix a cable close to the detector, but because he didn't have enough sleep he stumbled and fell. Of course he didn't tell anyone - but can you find out the exact time when it happened? Can you tell what has happened, maybe even (roughly) where he was working? Only that much: it happened during the period of time that you have data for.

You should look at the Z position vs. the time. The selector TBeamSpotSel already stores that in the histogram hBeamSpotZ_Time, so just plot that. As we said in the lecture, plotting should be done in Terminate. We extract the histogram from the output list (instead of just using fBeamSpotZ_Time) because that is how we can extract results obtained via PROOF.

When looking at the distribution of the Z position you will notice a sudden change at a certain time stamp. This means the the beam position as seen by the experiment has moved. A possible cause could be the student.

Could he have moved the beam? Unlikely.

Could he have moved the experiment? Maybe - if it's not too big... So where would he have to be standing to move the experiment in Z by falling? In the Z position next to the experiment!

Do you manage to find out exactly when the first and last event occurred?
